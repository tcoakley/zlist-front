About zChecklist

zChecklist started somewhere between campgrounds, highways, and coffee shops.

For five years I lived and worked full-time on the road as a software developer in a Winnebago View. It was one of the best periods of my life. But despite moving constantly, one thing never became stress free: departure day.

Every time I packed up to leave, there was always that moment:

  • Did I unplug the power?
  • Did I secure everything inside?
  • Did I disconnect the water?
  • Did I unplug the weBoost antenna wire?

On three separate occasions, I found myself pulling over just a few miles down the road wondering if I had forgotten that exact wire.

I searched many times for a checklist app that worked the way I needed. I wanted something designed specifically for tasks that repeat over and over again — not a grocery list, not a project board, not a corporate workflow system. Just a clean, reliable repeatable checklist.

I never really found one.

So eventually I decided:

one day I'll build it myself.

And this is that project.

As I see it, there are really three different kinds of lists:

1. Disposable Lists

These are things like grocery lists or one-time task lists:

  • create it
  • check things off
  • throw it away

These already exist everywhere and are built into many calendar and productivity systems.

2. Repeatable Checklists

This is what zChecklist is built for.

These are processes you repeat again and again:

  • packing an RV
  • pre-flight checks
  • deployment steps
  • camping setup
  • server maintenance
  • event preparation
  • monthly closing tasks
  • moving procedures

The important thing is confidence:

knowing every important step was completed.

To the best of my knowledge, this specific style of lightweight repeatable checklist system is surprisingly rare on the internet.

So I built the version I always wanted.

3. Daily Habit / Maintenance Lists

Then there are the things you do every single day.

For me, that is physical therapy and mobility work to keep my aging developer body functioning properly after decades behind keyboards.

Last I checked, nobody had built a version of that I really liked either.

So that may become a future project someday — or maybe eventually part of zChecklist itself.

zChecklist is intentionally simple.

It is not trying to become:

  • a giant enterprise platform
  • a social network
  • an AI productivity ecosystem
  • a surveillance-driven advertising machine

It is just meant to be a practical tool that helps people avoid forgetting important things.

Sometimes that is enough.
